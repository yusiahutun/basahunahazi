Lana Del Rey's ex-boyfriend ended the relationship after seeing where she lived

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, February 12, 2024

Lana Del Rey may be one of the most influential singer-songwriters of the 21st Century, but even that may not be enough for some people. She has now revealed one of her relationships with an ex-boyfriend came to an end because of the modesty of her home.

The music star rose to fame in 2011 with her debut single Video Games and has since gone from strength to strength, providing us with hit singles Summertime Sadness and Say Yes To Heaven.

Lana Del Rey’s ex-boyfriend called it quits over her house

The star sat down in an interview at her home with Harper’s Bazaar, however, revealed that one of her relationships ended for exactly that reason. Her home.

The interviewer explains how the house is ‘charming’ but “exceedingly modest for a woman of Del Rey’s fame and resources,” and some people just didn’t understand that, including one of Lana Del Ray’s ex-boyfriends.

“We had some fights over this house, a couple people. They didn’t get it,” she revealed.

It’s not the only modesty the star has shown, as earlier this year she made headlines for working a shift at Waffle House.

“I feel like even the most chill guy doesn’t really want to chill here,” the 38-year-old told the publication. “Sadly, part of you knows… that ain’t it.…That one shocked me. I won’t name names and whatever, but that one really shocked me, that person. That was actually the end of a relationship.”

As for now, the star reveals she’s “definitely not in love right now.” At the moment, it seems like her mind may be elsewhere.

“Well, I’ll tell you. It hasn’t crossed my mind in the last five months on the road or here yet. But give it a week. My history, sure, it’s coming for me at some point.

“Yeah. It would be interesting if it didn’t. It would be interesting if it didn’t,” she explains.

The star recently donated ‘every dollar’ from her tour

The star has been on a string of live shows this year, and while performing in Charleston, West Virginia on October 5, the singer told fans she’d donated the money earned back into cities she had performed in.

She told the crowd that “it wasn’t about the money” for her, and tours because she loves it, not because she needs to.

“I just wanted to say thank you to you and just every city I’ve gone to before this… every ticket, every dollar, it is poured right back into the city,” she announced on stage.
