    Now that we have discussed the top choices, you need to know on what basis you should buy that particular conditioner. To easily distinguish the best from the rest, we mentioned a few points below to keep in mind before choosing the best conditioner for color-treated hair. 

    How to Choose the Best Conditioner for Color-Treated Hair?

    Scroll down for our keynotes on how to choose the best conditioner from the rest. 

    • Key Ingredients: It's always sunny in haircare town when the ingredients tend to be on the natural side. With nourishing oils and hair growth essentials like coconut oil, argan oil, biotin, and keratin actives being present in formulas make the conditioner an ideal choice. It's a bonus when the product itself turns out to be plant-based ( vegan) and sustainably sourced as it's not only good for hair care but also the environment. 
      Sharing a piece of advice, our contributor William Slator, Product Developer at HairGuard - a brand specializing in hair growth products, says, “Individuals with sensitive skin may be allergic to certain ingredients like fragrance, preservatives, or specific botanical extracts found in conditioners. It's essential to patch-test new products and choose hypoallergenic or fragrance-free options if you have known sensitivities.”
    • Hair Color: It is vital to remember that the appropriate products for you will be determined by the sort of color treatment you have. If you have highlights, search for products that can enhance your color, but if you have darker shades, look for products that will protect and moisturize your hair. 
      Our contributor Mr. Slator shares, “Conditioners designed for color-treated hair, often labeled as color-protect or color-safe, are suitable for global color treatments. These conditioners help maintain the vibrancy of the color, prevent fading, and provide essential moisture to keep the hair healthy.”
    • Efficiency of Results: Also, when selecting your conditioner be sure to check out how long the conditioner helps to sustain the hair color on the strands. If the conditioner helps to increase the vibrancy and keeps the strands well-maintained without frizzing up, then you shouldn't skip out on it.
    • No Nasties: We always recommend keeping an eye out for harsh chemicals like alcohol, allergens, synthetic fragrances, dimethicone, formaldehyde, and retinyl palmitate that can cause build-up at your hair roots, and irritate or burn your scalp. Always check your labels for these pesky chemicals and be sure to opt for lean formulas that don't involve these ingredients at all. 

    Note: It always helps when the product is hypoallergenic, making it suitable for all hair types and delicate scalps.
