Yo Gotti Net Worth 2024: Income, Salary, Cars, Real Estate

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, May 23, 2024

Yo Gotti is an American rapper and record executive who has a net worth of $16 million. Gotti is the founder of the record label Collective Music Group as a record executive. He is considered one of many hardcore rappers that emerged from hip-hop’s Dirty South School in the late 1990s. He went on to release 11 studio albums, 22 mixtapes, and 56 singles (+ 21 as a featured artist), as well as 5 promotional singles.

Early Life

On May 19, 1981, Mario Mims was born in Memphis, Tennessee. His parents, uncles, and aunts were all hustlers. He grew up in a middle-class family. He was raised in the notoriously crime-ridden RidgeCrest housing projects.

Police invaded their home when Mario was in second grade, and his parents and other family members were jailed. As a result of having to fend for himself, he began tugging on the streets.

He began rapping at the age of 14 with his crew in Memphis throughout the 1990s. In 1996, he released his first Gangsta style Underground album entitled ‘Youngsta On A Come Up’ as ‘Lil Yo’ on the ‘Crime Lordz Records’ label.

The album quickly became a local hit, establishing Yo Gotti as the leader of a local underground hip-hop group and one of Memphis’ most talented rappers as well helped him gain his popularity.

Career

Between 2000 and 2006, Gotti released mixtapes such as Da Dope, Game 2 Da Rap Game, Self-Explanatory, Life, and Back 2 Basics, which earned positive reviews but not enough attention.

In 2012, he released his debut album Live From The Kitchen, which featured famous artists such as Rick Ross, Nicki Minaj, Gucci Mane, and others. The album peaked at number 12 on the US Billboard 200.

It spawned three top 100 singles, 5 stars, Women Lie Men Lie, and Look in the Mirror. In 2010, Gotti secured a licensing deal with Epic Records. His first single, Act Right, peaked at number 5 on the Bubbling Under Hot 100 singles chart.

Under the label, he would release four studio albums: I Am, The Art Of Hustle, I Still Am, and Untrapped. Collective Music Group, originally known as Cocaine Muzik Group, is a record label firm formed by Yo Gotti in the year 2000.

Between 2014 and 2017, Gotti released mixtapes under the CMG record label such as The Return, 2 Federal, and Chapter One White Friday (CM 9). In collaboration with Epic Records and CMG, he published his debut album, The Art Of Hustle.

It debuted in fourth place on the Billboard 200 charts and first place on the US Top R&B/ Hip-Hop Albums in 2016. Down in the DM’, the debut single, peaked at 13th on the US Billboard Hot 100 list.

Untrapped, Yo Gotti’s tenth studio album, was released in 2020 and achieved his 4th successive top-ten debut on the Billboard 200. Under a new contract with Epic, he released the single, Recession Proof on his label CMG.

Yo Gotti’s 11th album, CM10: Free Game, was released in 2021. It is a double-disc album comprising the discs Free and Game. Throughout his career, he has collaborated with numerous hip-hop artists to produce an upbeat hip-hop sound.

Real Estate

Gotti also invests immensely in real estate. He has been buying and selling estates since his music career took off. Gotti recently spent $7.6 million for a Tuscan-style mansion in Westlake Village, Calif built originally in 1999.

It features over 10,000 sq ft of living space 6 bedrooms, 8 bathrooms, and various other amenities. The home’s backyard features an infinity pool. There is also a tennis court, a playground, and a formal garden.
