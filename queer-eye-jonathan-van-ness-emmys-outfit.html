Queer Eye's Jonathan Van Ness Wears Dress To Creative Arts Emmys and Shuts Down Haters

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, February 23, 2024

Netflix's Queer Eye star Jonathan Van Ness has absolutely no time for haters. The grooming guru strutted the red carpet at the Creative Arts Emmy's last night in a Maison Margiela mesh and sequin high slit dress, Prada clutch, and Pierre Hardy platform bootiesâ€”and he absolutely killed the look.

"We absolutely came to slay this lewk & f**k a gender norm," he wrote on an Instagram post showing off his outfit.

But, when a few social media trolls weren't feeling his ensemble, JVN was quick to shut them down.

JC Olivera//Getty Images

One user commented, "The guy in the dress looks like a villain in despicable me 3." But JVN replied, "I look f**ing stunning and my name is Jonathan, snap chat filter using on your insta tiny circle private profile having self." @CommentsByCelebs captured the epic clap back.

Van Ness also went on to call out and denounce the apparent transphobia in the criticisms against his outfit choice. "Also I love these transphobic comments in here," he wrote. "'The guy in a dress' is insulting and for anyone that doesn't see the implications is blind. F**cking rood [sic] people I'm too tired."

Instagram

But JVN didn't let the negativity ruin his achievements. He and his four Queer Eye costars (Bobby Berk, Karamo Brown, Antoni Porowski, and Tan France) won an Emmy for Outstanding Structured Reality Program and Outstanding Casting for a Reality Program at the awards.

JC Olivera//Getty Images

There are two seasons of Queer Eye on Netflix, which is a reboot of the original program, Queer Eye for the Straight Guy, which first premiered in 2003. The "Fab 5" work to makeover a person's life by improving their self care through help with interior design, cooking, grooming, fashion, and culture.
