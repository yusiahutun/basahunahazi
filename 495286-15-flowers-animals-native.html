15 flowers that look like animals and where they are native

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, March 26, 2024

Flowers are some of the most beautiful living organisms on earth. There are over 400,000 flowering plant species worldwide, with flowers coming in different sizes, shapes, and colours. However, they are some unusual flowers that look like animals, humans, fairies, and aliens.

Why do some flowers look like animals and other creatures? For some, it is a survival technique. A flower mimics a bird or insect to attract it for pollination. For others, it is just a rare but exciting and beautiful coincidence.

15 flowers that look like animals

Nature is full of surprises. From the variety of geographical mechanisms to the organisms living in it – nature is astounding to study. Below are the top 15 flowers that look like animals and various other creatures.

1. Monkey flower

Dracula simia, also known as monkeyflower or monkey-face orchid, is one of the most unusual plants that resemble animals. It is known for its unique flowers that look like monkeys' faces.

The plant is native to the southeastern region of Ecuador and parts of Peru and mainly grows in the tropical forests of these Latin American countries.

2. Parrot flower

Parrot flower (Impatiens psittacina) is an orchid-like flowering plant from the balsam family. It's famous for its unique flowers, resembling parrots, hence the parrot flower.

The petals of the flowers have an uncanny likeness to the body of the parrot, with the hook of the flower looking like a bird's beak. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, commonly growing in Thailand, northeastern India, and Myanmar forests.

3. Rabbit succulents

Monilaria obconica is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa and Namibia. While the plant does not have flowers, its leaves resemble rabbit ears, giving it an adorable appearance.

It is thus one of the few succulents that look like animals and is known by several local names such as Bunny Ears, Rabbit Succulent, and Bunny Succulent.

4. Moth orchid

Phalaenopsis orchid, also known as the moth orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family. They have short leafy stems with flowers that bloom on branching flowering stems.

However, what's unique about this flower is its striking resemblance to a moth. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, China, Australia, and New Guinea.

5. Bee orchids

Bee orchids (scientific name Ophrys apifera) is a flowering plant from the orchid family. The plant is famous for its flowers, whose velvety lips resemble a female bumble bee. The flower also produces a sweet-smelling scent, similar to a female bee.

The flower is one of the top examples of sexually deceptive pollination – the striking resemblance to bees (and the scent it produces) lures male bees in for mating, thus helping to pollinate the flower. The plant is native to Europe but is also found in other regions, such as the Mediterranean and northern Iran.

6. Dove orchids

Peristeria elata is a flowering plant from the orchid family. It is known by several names, such as dove orchid, Holy Ghost orchid, and the flower of the Holy Spirit.

The reason for this is because of the plant's unique white blooms, whose inner petals closely resemble a dove. The plant is native to the Latin American region, where it commonly grows in Costa Rica, Trinidad, Panama, Venezuela, and Ecuador.

7. Flying duck orchids

Caleana major is an orchid plant native to eastern and southern Australian regions. It is commonly known by local names large duck orchids and flying duck orchids.

This is because of the plant's red, green, and purple flowers, which resemble a duck in flight. The flower is another example of sexually deceptive pollination, as it attracts insects that pollinate the flower.

8. The naked man orchid

The Italian orchid, popularly known as the naked man orchid, is one of the most unusual plants that look like humans. It is a species of the orchid family, which is native to the Mediterranean region and is commonly found in southwestern and southeastern Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

The plant has unique blooms, whose lips resemble a naked man – hence the local name.

9. White egret orchid

The white egret orchid (Pecteilis radiata) is another orchid that looks like an animal. It is native to China, Korea, Japan, and Russia. The plant has unique and rare white flowers, which resemble an egret. It also features fringed petals on opposite sides, making the flower look like an egret in flight.

10. Dolphin succulent

The Dolphin succulent (Senecio peregrinus) is a succulent plant native to the arid areas of southwestern Africa. It is famous for its leaves, which look like little dolphins jumping out of the water, creating an eye-catching display.

Due to this, the plant has several local names such as dolphin necklace, dolphin string, flying dolphins, and dolphin plant.

11. Flower lips

Flower lips (Palicourea elata) is a flowering plant native to the central and south American regions such as Mexico, Panama, Colombia, Ecuador, and Costa Rica. The plant is famous for its exquisite bright red flower that looks like a woman’s lips (before it fully blooms).

Due to this, the plant has some unusual local names, such as girlfriend’s kiss, hot lips, and ho*ker lips.

12. Fuchsia

Fuchsia are a genus of flowering plants, primarily small trees and shrubs. They are famous for their unique flowers, which look like dancing fairies. The plants are native to South America but also found in regions like Central America, New Zealand, and Tahiti.

13. Dancing ladies

Dancing ladies (Oncidium) are a genus of orchids which are native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants produce flowers in various shades, from dark brown to purple, white, and yellow. The flower petals look like small dancing ladies with bright skirts, hence their local name.

14. Green bird flower

The green bird flower (Crotalaria cunninghamii) is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia. It is also another one of the rare plants that look like birds. When blooming, the plant produces green petals whose arrangement gives them a striking resemblance to that of a bird’s wings and body.

The base and stem of the flower also have a striking resemblance to a bird's head and beak. Due to this, the plant is known by several local names such as hummingbird flower, regal bird flower, and parrot pea.

15. Happy alien flowers

The happy Alien plant (Calceolaria uniflora) is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America. It has unique orange-yellow flowers.

Each flower also sports a white band across the top and bottom petals, creating a mouth-like appearance. The unusual and out-of-this-world appearance of the flowers is why they earned the local name, happy alien flowers.
