Is it illegal to have a BBQ on a balcony? Heres what the law says

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, June 11, 2024

With summer fast approaching and temperatures rising, it’s understandable many will be wanting to start using their barbecues.

And with Prime Minister Boris Johnson giving the all-clear to have six people together for BBQs, subject to social distancing of course, now seems like the perfect time.

While not everyone is lucky enough to have a garden, some are lucky enough to have a balcony.

But with BBQs comes the question of safety and neighbourly etiquette.

Even more so when you live in a mid or high-rise complex where occupants live close to each other.

Many will no doubt be wondering if they are allowed to have a BBQ on their terraces and balconies.

Is it illegal to have a BBQ on a balcony?

There is no specific law against having a barbecue on your balcony, but you are highly advised against having one.

There is however a law when it comes to the use of common land, and town and public greens.

Some areas, including spaces owned by local council, privately, or by National Trust, have different regulations.

By-laws can be in place, which means wherever you are you should check if you are allowed.

Also, if you live in an apartment building the management have a right to make rules prohibiting BBQs on balconies for safety reasons.

London Fire Brigade say you should not be having barbecues on balconies.

Recently they have been pleading with people not to cause a balcony blaze which could displace hundreds of people as weather improves.

Many balconies have combustible materials, and there are often combustible items stored on them – so fires can spread very quickly.

London Fire Brigade has also, in recent years, seen a surge in balcony barbecues.

Deputy Commissioner Richard Mills said: “These balcony barbecues are a staggeringly irresponsible idea and are being marketed carelessly, with hardly any thoughts as to how dangerous they are.

“They are an accident waiting to happen and they are even being advertised with images of wooden balconies and with the suggestion customers have a fire extinguisher nearby.”

He added: “Don’t be the reason you and your neighbours end up looking for somewhere else to live because of your cigarette or barbecue.”

On London Fire Brigade’s website, it says: “Never barbecue on your balcony.

“Embers could carry and set alight to your or your neighbours balcony and possessions”.

If you’re planning a barbecue it’s important to place it on level ground, well away from anything that could catch fire, such as fences, overhanging trees and sheds.

Or in this case, your own, and other people’s balconies.
