The 12 Best Scar Creams of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, March 21, 2024

Scars are a fact of life; we all have them. Some of us wear them proudly as a sign of strength,  but if you don’t want yours to be front and center on your skin, it’s equally brave to choose to move on from them. And for that random gash you got from running into a tree branch as a kid, we’d bet you’re probably less passionate about keeping that forever reminder of your clumsiness (no judgment — we have them, too).

Whatever your reason, we’re here to help. Minimizing a scar is not as straightforward as getting rid of a small pimple or evening out a rough complexion, so we decided to test more than 30 of the most popular over-the-counter scar creams available on our own scars for three months to see if our markings became lighter or fainter.  We found that some scar creams were better at healing recent markings, while others took on the tough challenge of making old scars disappear. We took loads of photos from the very first day to the very last day of the three month test to track our progress and accurately observe how our scars progressed each week. We also made sure to note if the application triggered any uncomfortable or tingly sensation, or required far too many applications a day. Once we collected the data and tallied the scores, we curated this list of the very best scar creams out there so you can make your own ancient history (if that’s what you want to do).

What to Keep in Mind

Application process

You may have noticed that there are several different forms of scar treatments on this list. Some, like the Biodermis Epi-Derm C-Strip Package and the Rejûvaskin Scar Fx Silicone Sheeting, come in the form of a sheet that you peel and stick to the affected area. Consider whether or not you are ready to leave an adhesive sticker on for (more or less) 24 hours a day, and whether the scar is located in an area where this may be difficult to do. If you are the forgetful type, these types of treatments offer you a stress-free experience as you are only required to switch out the sticker every few days, and they feel nearly invisible.

Others come in the form of a gel, but even gels vary in their application processes. The HealFast Physician Formulated Medical-grade Silicone Scar Gel asked to be used three times a day, which meant we needed to carry it around if we left the house during the day. As tedious as that may be, like the name suggests, the scar healed fast, making the time commitment rather worth it. Other gels, like the Mederma Advanced Scar Gel, only needed to be applied once a day, which may feel like a more universal solution.

Time to results

Patience is a virtue, especially where scar creams are concerned. Don’t write off a cream even if you don’t see results in two weeks. Many creams inform you that it can take anywhere between eight weeks and six months to see dramatic results. Board-certified dermatologist Divya Shokeen M.D. explains that the “results will vary depending on the severity of the scar and individual factors, but noticeable improvements may be observed within a few weeks to a few months of consistent use.”

Key Ingredients

Typically, scar treatments contain “ingredients like silicone, vitamin E, and hyaluronic acid, which help to hydrate the skin, promote collagen production, and reduce inflammation,” says Dr. Shokeen. However, if you are looking for specific treatments, like treating keloids, you want to look for ingredients that help reduce not just coloring, but texture as well. The Cimeosil Scar and Laser Gel, which contains polysiloxanes, or silicone, helps to flatten and soften even old scars.

If you want to fade dark marks, board-certified dermatologist Dendy Engelman M.D. suggests looking for an option that features active ingredients such as hydroquinone, kojic acid, phytic acid, l-ascorbic acid, or glycolic acid, as they brighten the skin and promote cellular turnover to reveal younger, healthier cells, fade dark spots, and improve overall skin tone. You may need to follow your scar removal regimen with a separate product to achieve both goals. Your Questions, Answered

How does scar cream work?

While different scar creams harness the power of a variety of ingredients, they typically work by “retaining moisture and hydrating the healing wound,” says NY-based board-certified dermatologic surgeon Jeremy Brauer M.D. “Scar creams create an environment that promotes and optimizes wound healing, especially collagen production. Those that contain silicone may act to create a protective effect seal over the scar,” he explains.

For old scars, ingredients like allantoin and cepalin, found in the Mederma Advanced Scar Gel help to soften the skin and promote new cell regrowth, which can reverse the appearance of old markings.

How do I avoid getting a scar?

The best way to treat a scar is to attack it at its source, meaning the minute you get a wound, start treating it correctly. This does not mean apply scar creams to an open wound, warns Dr. Shokeen. Instead, “cleanse the wound, provide a hydrating environment, and avoid sun exposure,” says Dr. Brauer. Simple moisturizers will do wonders for a wound. You want to avoid having it create a scab, as these tend to cause scarring more easily. Tend to your wound by keeping infections at bay and moisturizing it daily.

Are scar creams safe on all skin types?

Dr. Shokeen confirms that scar creams are generally safe for most skin types. However, if you have any specific concerns, “it is always advisable to consult a dermatologist or conduct a patch test before using any new product.” If you know yourself to have sensitive skin, Dr. Shokeen warns that “some scar creams may contain potentially irritating ingredients, so asking your doctor for a recommendation can always be beneficial.” Dr. Brauer chimes in that “fragrance-free and alcohol-free products will suit those with sensitive skin much better.” And of course, look for scar creams with moisturizing agents, like the Mederma PM Overnight Scar Cream, to avoid irritation.
