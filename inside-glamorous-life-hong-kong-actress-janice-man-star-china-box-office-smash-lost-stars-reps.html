Inside the glamorous life of Hong Kong actress Janice Man: the star of China box office smash Lost i

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, March 12, 2024

Hong Kong audiences were surprised and delighted recently when mainland Chinese drama Demi-Gods and Semi-Devils aired on TVB, starring Hong Kong actress Janice Man.

Man has also garnered a lot of attention across the border for starring in new cinematic release Lost in the Stars. Since its release on June 22, box office ticket sales have exceeded US$320 million, per Variety – making it the highest grossing domestic crime film of all time in China.

Man started out as a model at the age of 14 before her first foray into the film world. She delivered some of her most memorable performances in films such as Helios, Cold War 2 and The Midnight After, earning two Hong Kong Film Award nominations. In recent years, she’s been making a name for herself in mainland China with TV dramas including Tribes and Empires: Storm of Prophecy and The Legend of Zu.

Here’s how the Hong Kong actress has been balancing her thriving acting career with her love of fashion, and living a life of luxury on her own terms …

Model material

Man’s natural poise makes her a highly sought-after ambassador for luxury brands. She caught the attention of fashion giants Valentino, Louis Vuitton and Chanel, and partnered with skincare brands like SK-II and Estée Lauder.

Fashion forward

Man likes to experiment with bold and daring fashion, showcasing her versatility with looks that range from sporty and edgy to elegant and feminine. The actress stunned onlookers at the 2016 Hong Kong Film Awards with one of the event’s sexiest red carpet looks to date: an ultra revealing Versus by Versace black body-hugging dress.

When it comes to everyday fashion choices on social media, she continues to inspire the masses by effortlessly mixing and matching different styles with designer apparel.

Jet set living

An avid globetrotter, Man travels extensively not only for work and fashion weeks but also to explore new cultures and destinations. From a sun-soaked villa in Vietnam to the snow-capped mountains of Cappadocia and the ancient ruins of Pompeii, she clearly indulges her wanderlust with off-the-beaten-path experiences and a sophisticated sense of style.

Staying in shape

Despite her busy schedule, Man prioritises her health and well-being and always stays active to maintain a strong and toned physique. The fitness enthusiast told HK01 that she carefully curates her workout regime for best results, including slow jogging for a lean figure and planks to sculpt her curves. She can often be seen donning stylish sportswear while practising yoga too.

A home fit for a star

Man got married to her billionaire boyfriend in 2019 with an extravagant wedding, per HK01. According to Sky Post, the couple now lives in a duplex mansion in Tai Po’s prestigious Beverly Hill, which reportedly boasts a spacious living room with full-length windows, a stunning rooftop terrace and luxurious bathrooms, all featuring an elegantly minimalist design.
