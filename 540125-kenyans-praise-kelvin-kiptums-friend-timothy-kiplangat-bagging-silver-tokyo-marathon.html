Kenyans Praise Kelvin Kiptum's Friend Timothy Kiplagat for Bagging Silver in Tokyo Marathon

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, April 19, 2024
  • Timothy Kiplagat trained together with the late world record holder Kelvin Kiptum before his tragic demise
  • He clinched second place in 2:02:44 in the Tokyo marathon after Benson Kipruto, who won the gold medal after crossing the finish line in 2:02:16
  • Leaders have showered him with praises for bringing more light to the athletic fraternity despite partaking in the race without his partner

Amos Khaemba, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Kenyans have showered Timothy Kiplagat with praise after emerging second in the 2024 Tokyo Marathon.

Kiplagat was a close friend to the late Kelvin Kiptum, who used to train together for the marathon before the latter passed away in a tragic road accident.

In the race, Benson Kipruto finished first, while Vincent Ngetich secured the third spot with impressive times of 2:02:16 and 2:04:19, respectively.

Reaction as Kenyans shine at Tokyo Marathon

Transport Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen mentioned that the emerging champion secured a silver medal despite the emotional challenge following the death of his friend.

"Special congratulations to Timothy Kiplagat ( in Orange) who was training for Tokyo with the late Kelvin Kiptum (in blue), for finishing second despite the grief and pain of losing a friend and a training partner. Well done," Murkomen said.

Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua said:

"Our prowess in athletics has again been affirmed at the Tokyo Marathon this morning where Kenyans swept the podium in an enviable fashion. Congratulations Benson Kipruto for winning the Marathon in a course record of 2:02:16. Bravo Timothy Kiplagat and Vincent Kipkemoi Ngetich for finishing second and third, respectively. My plaudits also to Rosemary Wanjiru for coming in second in the women's race."

Gladys Boss Shollei wrote:

"Congratulations, Benson Kipruto, on your incredible victory at the Tokyo Marathon! You ran an impressive time of 2:02:16 and showed true determination and skill. Many congratulations also to Timothy Kiplagat and Vincent Kipkemoi Ng'etich for coming 2nd and 3rd, respectively."

Henry Kabogo observed:

"Benson Kipruto, the Tokyo Marathon winner, training with the Late Kelvin Kiptum, the world record holder. The duo have defeated the former world record holder Eliud Kipchoge in the recent races."

Johny Leon said:

"Thanks to Timothy for representing your friends especially Kiptum, may you rest in peace bro."

Kenyans advise Kipchoge to retire

Meanwhile, a section of Kenyans advised the world-renowned marathoner Eliud Kipchoge to retire from athletics after finishing 10th in the marathon.

The group argued that Kipchoge had made records in his previous performances and would remain a hero.

According to them, the celebrated Olympian would not have managed a gold medal for several reasons, including age.
