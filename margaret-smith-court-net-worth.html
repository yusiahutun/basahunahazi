Brief Glance on Margaret Smith Court

Margaret Smith Court (born 16 July 1942) is the youngest of four children of Lawrence Smith and Catherine Beaufort.

She grew up in a rough condition; however, today, she is known as  one of only three players in history to have won the “Grand Slam Boxed Set.”

To elaborate, she won all titles in the Grand Slam and is also one of only six tennis players ever to win a Multiple Grand Slam set in two disciplines.

Court is also the first lady player from Australia to win a Grand Slam tournament abroad. 

Apart from her professional side, Margaret is married to Barry Court, and they share four children. Moreover, she also has a sports and entertainment complex Melbourne Park named after her. 

It is named Margaret Court Arena; however, it was asked to change the name after Margaret’s words.

Back then, Margaret Smith Court gained loads of criticisms after opposing LGBT rights and same-sex marriage in Australia.

Due to the same reason, tennis players Billie Jean King, Rennae Stubbs, and Martina Navratilova also criticized her statements.
