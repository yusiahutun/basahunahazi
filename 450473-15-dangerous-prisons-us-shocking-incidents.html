While serving a prison sentence is not meant to be luxurious, some prisons are equipped with simple inmate privileges such as access to recreation fields, gyms, sports equipment and hearty meals. The prison lifestyle is not meant to be torturous, with conditions below those of human concern. However, some of the most dangerous prisons in the US have the most shocking incidents.

The most dangerous prisons in the US have proven that prisons are not an ideal place to spend time. They are a place not just for the punishment conditions in which the inmates live but also for themselves. If a place has taken responsibility to correct a person, they should abide by this.

1. United States Penitentiary, Administrative Maximum Facility

  • Location: Fremont County, near Florence, Colorado
  • Security-class: Administrative Maximum
  • Population: 343
  • Famous inmates: Zacarias Moussaoui, Khalfan Mohamed, Umar Abdulmutallab and Sulaiman Abu Ghaith

Commonly known as the ADX, United States Penitentiary, Administrative Maximum Facility is one of the worst maximum-security prisons in the United States. It has three correctional facilities: level II, level V, and Level Vi.

ADX has experienced some of the worst prison riots in USA history. In 1980, the innate took over the prison taking 12 officers hostage while killing 33 inmates. Over the years, inmates have filed uncountable class-action lawsuits about extreme and unnatural punishment at ADX.

Twin Towers Correctional Facility is considered a bigger and more threatening facility in the United States. It is home to some of the world's worst criminals, and with the increased population, criminal rates to go up by the day.

The United States Penitentiary Beaumont is considered one of the most dangerous prisons due to the number of murder plots in the facility. Even though it is a maximum-security prison, the inmates plan out killing, as witnessed in 2007 and 2008.

10. Rikers Island Prison

Rikers Island is one of the worst state prisons in America. The facility is characterized by inmate violence, staff brutality and abuse of the mentally ill and adolescents.
