Ethan Macharia: Boy Who Could Suffer Upto 40 Seizures Daily Recovers After President Uhuru Paid KSh

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, August 31, 2024
  • Ethan Macharia fell headfirst from a building while he was playing with friends back in 2019
  • Because of the fall, he started having about 40 convulsions in a single day; a clear sign he needed advanced treatment
  • The treatment cost of his condition amounted to over KSh 3.5 million and which President Kenyatta cleared, paving the way for treatment in India

Ethan Macharia, the boy who fell head-first from a building damaging his head badly, is recovering well.

Violent seizures and over KSh 3.5m bill

Aged three years at the time of the accident, little Ethan would develop up to 40 violent seizures per day after hurting his head, causing his mum to seek ways of getting better treatment for him.

While on a recent visit to the boy’s family, Public Relations practitioner and philanthropist Kevin Otiende said his mum reached out to him in November of 2019, asking for help.

Special surgery in India

Despite their best effort to raise the over KSh 3. 5 million required for him to have special surgery in India, they could not raise it.

“Nothing seemed to work. When I first visited the boy, he literally suffered a seizure immediately I arrived. It was bad. One day, we wrote a letter to President Uhuru Kenyatta, and he literally cleared the boy's bill on the same day he got the letter,” Otiende said.“The surgery was successful; he now has a special device implanted in his heart that stops the attacks. I was thrilled to find him healthy, doing his homework, before we shared a meal,” Otiende added.

According to Otiende, the boy’s life is now fully back to normal. And God, indeed, sits on His throne.

Macharia's Emotional letter to Uhuru

As previously reported, Ethan penned a heartfelt letter to the president with the aid of his mother, Veronica Njeri detailing how he fell while playing with his friends. Kenya's ambassador to India Willy Bett also confirmed that Uhuru had footed the bill as Macharia was set to undergo the Vagus Nerve Simulator surgery.

The family spent over KSh 1.5 million for treatment in different hospitals in the country since the boy fell down.
