Elie Saab interview | Elixir perfume fragrance launch

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, October 6, 2024

Elie Saab believes in magic. Not in a Harry Potter, wand-waving sort of way, but magic in the form of fairy-tale storytelling and making dreams come true. I'm in Paris to meet the designer in his private couture salon; a space above his boutique on Avenue Franklin Delano Roosevelt, where VIP clients enjoy personal consultations and fittings. It's a surprisingly minimal space, given the brand's reputation for glitz and glamour, with black high-shine floors, stark white walls and angular furniture. The magic comes via several mannequins dressed in spectacular lace and sequin couture gowns, with feathered trains, embellished bodices and glittering sleeves; inspiration for the ultra high-net-worth customers that adore Saab's princess-like creations. If Cinderella didn't have a fairy godmother, this is surely where she'd come.

"We create dreams, honestly," he tells me, gesturing to his designs. Behind him a video of the brand's latest autumn/winter 2023 show plays on a loop via a screen built into a mirror, an endless parade of elegance and aspiration. These are gowns that royals choose for state dinners; that the super-rich party in; that socialites wear on their wedding day.

The latest spell that the designer is casting comes in the form of "a love potion" called Elixir, his brand's newest fragrance – an alluringly spicy floral scent fronted by the supermodel Candice Swanepoel. "There is something magical about the brand," agrees Nathalie Helloin Kamel, CEO of the Luxury Fragrance Division at Give Back Beauty, which co-produces Elie Saab Parfums. "When you wear something from Elie Saab you feel like you have [a kind of] power, and we think you can extend that power to a love potion." She describes the process of creating the perfect perfume as similar to the way that someone might search for a soulmate. "When people talk about their first encounter with their favourite fragrance, it’s like they met their lover. Really, it’s a love story. And they love when they enter a room that people recognise their scent."

When it comes to having a signature scent, Saab himself believes that there's nothing more "elegant" than having a recognisable fragrance: "It should be part of you." In fact, the brand's research found that 50 per cent of beauty consumers are looking for "the one". The other 50 per cent "consider fragrance as a fashion accessory, and it changes according to their mood or occasion", explains Helloin Kamel. "It's very emotional and personal."

But the Elixir scent is as much about conjuring a mood as it is an accessibly priced way of buying in to the otherwise extremely exclusive Elie Saab world. "This is a product that anyone can have," Saab tells me, acknowledging that his fashion designs fall outside of most shoppers' budgets. "But whether you buy a small perfume or a ten-thousand euro dress, it’s the same feeling: it makes people feel special." Achieving "that feeling", he says, is ultimately what motivates him. "When a woman is happy, everyone can feel that she’s happy," he explains. "When I meet a woman and she’s wearing my clothes or [my fragrance] and she’s happy, that’s enough for me. It’s a lot of responsibility... but I work for that. I don’t need more than that."

"If we didn't have our brand signature, we would not be here now"

When it comes to retaining customer loyalty, Saab is something of an expert. Having launched his eponymous label in 1982, the designer has since become a regular fixture on the Paris Fashion Week schedules – both ready-to-wear and couture. His gowns are instantly recognisable in their execution; a signature style that he has honed over years of practice.

"If we didn't have our signature, we [would not be] here now," he says. "We might be copied [by other brands], but our client knows that and still comes to see us. We have our DNA." It's a style that has rarely changed and to lucrative effect; while other fashion houses might resort to flashy gimmicks or headline-making stunts, Elie Saab has taken the quietly confident approach – consistently producing beautiful dresses that his customers love wearing. "We are a timeless brand," he agrees. "We always think long-term. It’s not about fashion lasting six months, it’s not seasonal – it’s for life." It's an attitude that feels distinctly 'now' – in keeping with the post-pandemic, anti-trend mood where shoppers want to invest in classic, ageless fashion.

Helloin Kamel concurs that considering customer loyalty is at the heart of all decision-making within the company. "The most challenging element is not to sell a product once; because of the beautiful story of Elie Saab, it’s pretty easy to sell one bottle," she says. "But what will make the woman come back? It’s the product, it’s the fragrance, it's the way it makes her feel and it’s the kind of comments she’s going to receive. It’s the same when you wear a beautiful dress and you receive compliments. When you wear a beautiful fragrance and you receive positive comments from people around you, it’s amazing. If people tell you you smell good then for sure you’re going to buy that [fragrance] again!"

"It’s not about fashion lasting six months, it’s not seasonal – it’s for life"

Ultimately, as a master storyteller, Saab has managed to create an entire fantasy world; a luxury mindset that his customers buy into."It’s all about the story around the brand," the designer says. "You might see in the newspaper a princess wearing an Elie Saab gown, or someone getting married in an Elie Saab wedding dress, but it’s the small things that you can [actually] buy." He pauses, before adding: "It all starts with a story." Long may the Elie Saab fairy-tale continue.
