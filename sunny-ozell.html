Early Life

Sunny Ozell grew up in a musical household in Reno, Nevada, and began learning the violin at age 4 with her parents’ support. At 11 years old, she began taking vocal lessons and soon realized that music was her career. At 13, she started working with an opera coach to pursue her passion further.

She studied English Literature and used her spare time to hone her singing skills in blues bands and soul-funk groups at the University of Colorado Boulder. After graduating, Ozell moved to New York City with dreams of becoming a professional musician.

She quickly found success there, performing regularly in clubs. Her performances featured both original compositions as well as interpretations of classic works by other renowned songwriters. As such, she became an integral part of New York’s musical community.

Sunny Ozell Career

Sunny Ozell is a renowned singer who started her career in New York performing in clubs. She released her debut album, Take It With Me, and has worked with musicians like Howard Jones and Hank Williams.

Her style of music is truly unique and mostly influenced by the works of renowned musicians such as Gillian Welch and David Rawlings. She specializes in singing a variety of genres, including Country music, Jazz music, Americana music, blues, and country pop. Her most recent musical offerings include Driving Highways (2019), a heartfelt track that showcases her emotive vocal range, as well as All That I Am (2020).

Ozell is a frequent performer at Rockwood Music Hall and The Living Room in New York City, where she has had the opportunity to collaborate with some of the most talented musicians around. She has worked with Jim Campilongo, Adam Levy of Norah Jones’ backing band, as well as Krystle Warren; all three have been instrumental in helping her craft her unique sound.
