WWE star suffers ANOTHER wardrobe malfunction on live television in match on SmackDown... as produce

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, June 4, 2024

WWE star Tiffany Stratton suffered another wardrobe malfunction during her match against Zelina Vega on SmackDown.

The 24-year-old, who is dating fellow WWE wrestler Ludwig Kaiser in real life, has recently been promoted to the main roster following a string of impressive performances on developmental brand NXT.

She qualified for this weekend's Elimination Chamber match in Perth on Friday on SmackDown, beating Zelina Vega following interference from Elektra Lopez.


But all did not go completely to plan, as Stratton suffered a wardrobe malfunction early on in the match, forcing the producers to take action on the live television broadcast.

Early on, the screen cut to black as Vega climbed to the corner to perform a moonsault, before the screen cut to black and returning to the action moments later.

WWE star Tiffany Stratton suffered a wardrobe malfunction on live television on SmackDown

The issue happened when Vega took to the second rope to perform a moonsault early on

Stratton performed the move well, but the screen soon cut to black before the camera panned away from her

When the action did return, however, Stratton remained off camera with Vega and referee Jessika Carr looking concerned before she returned to the ring.

Stratton experienced an issue with her bra following the move, causing the producers to temporarily cut the feed to make sure nothing more than intended was revealed.

She didn't seem to experience any further issues regarding the feed having to be cut for the rest of the match.

Unfortunately for Stratton, it wasn't the first time she had suffered such an issue in her short career, with the latest happening as little as two months ago at the NXT Deadline event.

Competing in an Iron Survivor match, Stratton's bra again came lose, resulting in the feed being cut for around five seconds before returning.

It was back to the routine on Monday, however, as the 24-year-old took part in a pre-Elimination Chamber segment

On RAW, three days after the mishap on SmackDown, things went a little more to plan as Stratton was involved in an in-ring segment with the other five women who have qualified for the Elimination Chamber match.

The stars spoke briefly on the microphone before a brawl broke out, with Nia Jax eventually clearing the ring of all performers, including Stratton.

Sunday will be Stratton's second appearance on a main roster premium live event following an impressive showing at last month's Royal Rumble.
