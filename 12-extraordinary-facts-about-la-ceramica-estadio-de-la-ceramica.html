FAQs

1. What is the capacity of La Cerámica?

La Cerámica has a seating capacity of over 23,000 spectators.

2. Can I take a tour of La Cerámica?

Yes, guided tours of La Cerámica are available, offering visitors the chance to explore the stadium and learn about its history and features.

3. How did La Cerámica get its name?

The stadium is named after the ceramics industry, which is significant in the region of Castellón, where the stadium is located.

4. Is La Cerámica a multi-purpose stadium?

No, La Cerámica is primarily used for football matches. However, it can also host other events such as concerts and exhibitions.

5. How can I get tickets for a match at La Cerámica?

Tickets for matches at La Cerámica can be purchased online or at the stadium’s ticket office. It is recommended to buy tickets in advance, especially for popular games.

6. Is La Cerámica accessible for people with disabilities?

Yes, La Cerámica is equipped with facilities to accommodate people with disabilities, including accessible seating areas and ramps.

7. What is the best time to visit La Cerámica?

The best time to visit La Cerámica is during a live match to experience the vibrant atmosphere. However, guided tours are available throughout the year.

8. Are there any nearby attractions to visit after a match at La Cerámica?

Yes, the stadium is located close to several attractions, including the El Madrigal Park and the Castellón Cathedral, making it convenient to explore the area after a match.
