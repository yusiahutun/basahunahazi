Best Clothing Rental Services of 2024

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, August 26, 2024

Clothing rental services allow you to flex your style without cluttering your closet or draining your wallet. If you’re craving a wardrobe refresh, you can try bold looks from premium designers without the hassles of upkeep or steep price tags. While clothing rental services are mostly associated with special occasion attire, you’ll find plenty of monthly services that cater to a wide style range. 

From chic work pieces to cute vacation fits, there is a bevy of options available. We took a deep dive into the best clothing rental services to help you find the right subscription for your preferences, no matter if you're into high-end luxury pieces or simply want accessories for your everyday looks. Prepare to elevate your closet when you sign up for one of these vetted clothing rental services.

Armoire

Best Clothing Rental Services of 2023

Best Overall: Rent the Runway

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $94-$235/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 3

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Can pick which items you want to return first

  • Thorough cleaning process uses biodegradable detergents

  • Can purchase items you love

Why It's a Top Pick

Rent the Runway is probably best known for special event rentals, but you can snag cute off-duty and in-the-office fits from its vast collection, too. There are over 750 designers to browse, including Tory Burch, Reformation, and Maje. Depending on which plan you sign up for, you can grab four to 16 pieces of clothing per month.

The more premium your subscription, the more expensive your individual items will be, but higher tiers do mean more monthly shipments. Of the three available options, the most-loved plan is the $144 per month plan, which allows you to pick up to eight pieces (worth up to $3,000 each) per month. 

Shipments, which come with four items, generally arrive in two days or less. With an active membership, you can hold onto your clothes for as long as you'd like, purchase clothes you love, and place one-time rentals. Getting the correct size is heavily reliant on customer reviews, but this can also work to your advantage as you can see through measurement details in these assessments.

Best for Vacation: Nuuly

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $88/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Can choose from over 150 brands

  • No damage or late fees for rentals

  • Helpful sorting filters for weather and occasion

Why It's a Top Pick

Impress in your cutest vacation fits with Nuuly. For $88 per month, access six pieces of clothing from over 150 brands — think picks from Anthropologie, Urban Outfitters, Kate Spade, Anna Sui, and Free People. There are no damage or late fees, which could come in handy if you have any vacation mishaps. All you have to do is return the item, and Nuuly will handle the cleaning and repairs. 

Nuuly’s website interface also makes it easy to use, since its filters go beyond basic size and color. The particularly helpful filters are for weather and occasion, which allow you to discover appropriate pieces for your destination, no matter if it’s warm and humid or cold and snowy. Plus, you can pause or cancel your subscription at any time so you can plan your wardrobe around your trips. However, once you get a box, you will have to commit to the pieces until your next box or pay for your next monthly delivery earlier.

Best for Photoshoots: Armoire

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $79-$249/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 3

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Thousands of brands available to select outfits from

  • Membership includes styling tips and services

  • Can cancel or pause membership any time

Why It's a Top Pick

Whether you've got a photoshoot for professional portraits or a fun event, Armoire will have you covered with clothing rentals and styling tips. When you sign up, you’ll answer questions about your style preferences so that the service can personalize your outfit with items from its collection containing over 1,000 brands. To get the just-right look for your photos, you can even consult an Armoire stylist with your membership.

The Unlimited plan allows you to have six pieces per shipment, and you can swap as often as you'd like. With the Capsule plan, you get four or seven items per monthly case. With any active membership, you can keep pieces for as long as you’d like. Do be sure to return your rented items on time, as the service charges late fees that can add more cost to its already pricey subscription.

Best for Men: Vince Unfold

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $160/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes 
  • No. of Plans: 1

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Minimalist style is ideal for casual and semi-casual wear

  • Flat monthly fee means you can receive and return as much as you’d like

  • Features premium materials like cashmere, suede, and silk

Why It's a Top Pick

If you’re looking for men’s clothing, Vince Unfold is a solid clothing rental subscription with a wide option range catering to more masculine and gender-neutral styles. The Los Angeles-based brand is known for its understated, minimalist-leaning luxury pieces made with materials like cashmere, suede, and silk. With items like henleys, jeans, and sweaters, its men’s selection is versatile enough for casual, semi-formal, and work occasions. 

When you sign up for a subscription ($160 a month), you can choose 10 or more items for your personal wish list, and receive four pieces at a time with complimentary free shipping. You can also return clothes at your own pace throughout the month, giving you some time and flexibility to try on different outfits. While Vince Unfold lacks in the shoes, accessories, and formal departments, the flat monthly fee for several pieces is hard to pass up.

Best Plus Size: Gwynnie Bee

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $49-$199/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 6

Pros & Cons 

Pros

  • Inclusive size range available, from 0 to 32

  • Clothes suit different styles and occasions

  • Can easily manage, cancel, or upgrade plan

Cons

  • Specific measurements for bust, hip, etc. are hard to pinpoint

  • Can only prioritize, not pick, what gets shipped

Why It's a Top Pick

Gwynnie Bee is our top contender for an apparel rental service with a comprehensive size range—you can find picks from sizes 0 to 32. Its site even lists what sizes are available in each of its available brands, which include Levi’s, Calvin Klein, Vince Camuto, and more. Plus, Gwynnie Bee has a guide called “The Shape Report,” which helps users browse styles and tips for their body type. The clothes themselves are also varied, as there are options for professional, casual, and event attire. 

Monthly subscriptions start at $49 per month. Pricing depends on how many items you have at a given time. You can have anywhere from one to 10 items, and it’s easy to adjust your plan as needed. However, you can only prioritize what you want to get delivered—meaning that you’ll have to decide which items you want first if you’re getting multiple pieces of clothing since shipping times can vary for individual pieces.

Best for Accessories: Vivrelle

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $39-$279/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 4

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Features luxury designers such as Chanel and Louis Vuitton

  • Insurance plans available for both normal wear and accidental damage

  • Can return items at your own pace

Why It's a Top Pick

If you're happy with your wardrobe but want to jazz up your accessories, Vivrelle is a convenient service for trying out jewelry and handbags. You can switch items monthly, although it’s possible to keep accessories for longer or buy them at a member-only price. Featured luxury brands include Saint Laurent, Chanel, Louis Vuitton, and many more.

The starting Premier plan ($39/month) will allow you to access one jewelry item worth up to $1,000 at a time. With the Couture+ subscription plan ($279/month), you can pick two pieces worth up to $4,000 at a time. Standard insurance is included for normal wear and tear, although there’s the option to add a monthly $15 insurance plan for accidents. Do note though, that there’s a mandatory three-month membership minimum, so signing up for this service will be a commitment.

Best for Work: Le Tote

Key Specs

  • Average Price: $59-$129/month
  • Free Shipping? Yes
  • No. of Plans: 2 plans; 16 options

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Specifically features an edit for workwear

  • Can choose one box a month or unlimited boxes

  • Can pause or cancel membership easily

Why It's a Top Pick

Le Tote is perfect for rounding out your 9-to-5 closet, whether you want clothes for every day of the week or statement pieces for special work meetings and events. In fact, Le Tote even has a collection called “The New 9 to 5” to help you find the best clothing rentals for work. Whether you’re into a classic sheath dress or want to try out a bold cropped blazer, you’ve got plenty of options. While you won’t find a lot of high-end names, you’ll find mid-range brands such as Max Studio and French Connection.

Plans at Le Tote start at $59 per month, and you can get one tote per month or unlimited totes per month (each tote comes with three or four clothing items and two accessories). With the unlimited subscription, it’s a good idea to strategically plan when to wear and return your clothes to maximize your time with them. Bear in mind that fulfillment takes about one to two days, and your tote will ship for about two to three days, so be sure to plan ahead if you need these clothes for an upcoming work event.

Best for Community: Tulerie

Key Specs

  • Average Price: Depends on lender
  • Free Shipping? Fee built into rental cost
  • No. of Plans: 3

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Company is focused on sustainability and connecting users

  • Can both borrow and lend pieces

  • Funds acquired by lending can go toward rentals

Why It's a Top Pick

Tulerie, a peer-to-peer luxury closet-sharing app, presents the perfect opportunity to bond with fellow fashion lovers who have picks from designers such as Gucci and Bottega Veneta. In addition to lending your personal collection, you can rent pieces that you’re interested in for four, 10, or 20 days. If you’re a lender, you can even use the money you earned for future rentals. 

Here are some things to keep in mind: Tulerie is free to join, but rental costs are up to the lender. There are also other fees to consider, including late return and cancellation fees. Plus, there is no standardized cleaning process, as lenders clean the garments themselves. Additionally, expect to be interviewed when applying since Tulerie thoroughly vets new members before joining the service.

Final Verdict

Rent the Runway is our top pick for the best clothing rental service because it offers choice, reliability, and convenience. You can choose from a wide range of designers, including niche and luxury brands, and—depending on your plan—you can even get items worth up to $3,000. 

While Rent the Runway is often associated with wedding and event attire, it also features cute fits to elevate your everyday looks. On matters concerning reliability and convenience, the service features fast free shipping, in addition to thorough and eco-friendly cleaning practices. Lastly, if you love a piece, you can keep it longer or buy it.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How Does Renting Clothes Work?

    With clothing rental services, you pay a fixed fee to receive a set amount of clothes for a period of time. The clothes are already cleaned, so all you have to do is wear them and return them so the company can launder the items again.

  • How Often Will You Receive Clothes From a Clothing Rental Service?

    How often you receive clothes from a rental service depends on your plan. Some subscriptions have a fixed fee for a set amount of clothes each month, which may arrive in one or multiple shipments. Other subscriptions feature an “unlimited” model where you can check out as many items as possible per month as long as you return clothes from previous deliveries.

  • Can You Buy Any of the Pieces You Receive Through a Clothing Rental Service?

    You can often buy the pieces you borrow from a clothing rental service. All you have to do is follow the company’s procedure for purchasing rented clothes. With a membership, you’ll likely receive a discount from an item’s typical retail price.

  • How Do You Return Items From a Clothing Rental Service After Wearing Them?

    Clothing rental services usually have an in-house process for cleaning garments, so chances are you won’t have to clean your pieces. When your rentals come to you, there’s usually a free shipping label included in the package or your account for returns. Let the service know which items you’re sending back, and ship your pieces when you’re ready.

  • Are Clothing Rental Services Cheaper Than Buying Clothes?

    Rental services can be expensive for savvy shoppers who have an eye for sales or those who shop for budget or mid-range brands. Still, rental subscriptions may be cheaper than buying clothes at retail prices if you’re looking for luxury brands. Rental services also take the cost out of any special dry cleaning since the clothes arrive clean and don’t need to be returned clean.

  • Are Clothing Rental Services Sustainable?

    Instead of pushing you to buy more products, rental services allow you to try out clothes that get circulated among subscribers. If you don’t love a piece, you can return it for someone else to enjoy instead of throwing it away and contributing to waste. Of course, it’s important to consider the environmental impact of shipping when renting clothes. If you’re checking out and returning pieces multiple times a month, this leaves a more significant carbon footprint than cycling through one batch of clothes per month.

Methodology

We reviewed more than a dozen clothing rental services to find the best ones for different types of customers. Each company was judged using the following criteria:

  • Average cost
  • Shipping fees
  • Clothing variety
  • Range of sizes
  • Delivery locations
  • Number of plan options
  • Rental restrictions
  • Added perks
  • Access to customer service

Companies with a wide range of clothing styles and available sizes were given extra points in the scoring phase. Cost was also a leading factor, as was customer support and additional services offered. Companies with limited shipping capabilities did not make it past the initial research stage.
