Amy Lee is a well-known singer and songwriter from the United States. Amy Lee is known as the lead vocalist and co-founder of the rock/metal band, Evanescence.

Moving on to her academic qualification, she went to Pulaski Academy, a private college preparatory school, starting in junior high. Later, she enrolled at Middle Tennessee State University.

Professional Career

However, Amy did not complete her graduation and formed a band named ‘Evanescence’ in 1995 after meeting Ben Moody, a lead guitarist, at a youth camp. This band performed on local radio stations until they released limited copies of two Extended Plays between 1998 and 1999, the first was the self-titled, “Evanescence EP”, and the second was the “Sound Asleep EP.”

In the year 2000, they recorded, ‘EP Origin’, with songs such as ‘Whisper’, ‘Imaginary’ and ‘My Immortal’. Because of their popularity, these songs, composed by the band’s founders, were included in a later album. Lee sang two songs as a guest singer for ‘Evanescence’ keyboardist David Hodge in the year 2000: ‘Breathe’ and ‘Fall Into You’. ‘Fall Into You’, on the other hand, was never released.

In 2003, she sang background vocals on ‘Missing You,’ a song by ‘Big Dismal,’ a Florida-based post-grunge Christian Rock band comprised of Eric Durrance, Chuck Shea, Gary Sobel, and Jeff Chomin. In 2003, she sang backup vocals for the rock supergroup ‘The Damning Well’ on two songs. ‘Awakening’ was released as the ‘Underworld’ movie soundtrack, while ‘Power’ was never released.

Other musical projects

In 2004, she recorded a duet with Shaun Morgan for the album ‘Disclaimer II’, released by the South African post-grunge band ‘Seether’, which was featured on the soundtrack of the film ‘The Punisher.’ In 2006, she made a cameo appearance in Johnny Cash’s ‘God’s Gonna Cut You Down’ music video. She sang a duet with Jonathan Davis of the ‘Korn’ nu metal band.

In 2008, she recorded a remake of ‘Sally’s Song’ for Walt Disney’s ‘Nightmare Revisited’. She performed the song live at the film’s premiere in Hollywood and on Jay Leno’s ‘The Good Night Show’. Her band’s self-titled album ‘Evanescence’ was released in 2011. It was the band’s most ambitious project to date. It was first released in Europe, and then five days later in the United States. She recorded ‘I’m So Lonesome I Could Cry’ in 2012 for the tribute album ‘We Walk The Line: A Celebration of Johnny Cash’s Music.

As well as this singer has provided soundtracks to the films War Story (2014) and Indigo Grey: The Passage (2015) with cellist Dave Eggar, and the song “Speak to Me” for the film Voice from the Stone (2017). Meanwhile, this singer also worked with artists like Korn, Seether, Bring Me the Horizon, and Lindsey Stirling.
