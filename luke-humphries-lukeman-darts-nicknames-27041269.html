Darts rivals once faced off to decide who kept nickname

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, April 28, 2024

Colourful nicknames are synonymous with darts. Picking a catching moniker is as big a part of the sport as choosing your arrows.

But what happens when two players pick the name nickname or something similar? That was the issue facing Luke Humphries and Martin Lukeman, who have both impressed on the European Tour this season.

Humphries has already won three titles while Lukeman reached the final of the German Grand Prix last month, losing to Humphries. A few years back, the pair were playing for more than just a trophy or prize money, they were playing for the right to keep their nickname. Their aliases weren’t quite identical – ‘Cool Hand Luke’ vs ‘Cool Man Luke’ – but they were considered close enough that one of them needed to be known as something else.

“I played Luke on the Challenge Tour and he’d changed his name to ‘Cool Hand Luke’ so we said whoever wins this match gets to keep the name, and he did me!,” Lukeman told the Weekly Dartscast Podcast last month.

Humphries, speaking to the same podcast last month, recalled: “That was about five or six years ago. I think it was the quarter-finals on the Challenge Tour. He was called ‘[Cool] Man Luke’ and I was called ‘[Cool] Hand Luke’. We both realised we couldn’t go on with the same sort of nickname. So he offered to play me for the nickname, which I did and I think I beat him 5-0 or 5-1.

“If it wasn’t for that game, I might not have been called ‘Cool Hand Luke’, but it was fate that I was. The nickname has stuck with me now and it suits my style of play.”

Forced to come up with something else, Lukeman promptly changed his nickname to ‘Smash’ after it was suggested by his wife He even has a tattoo in honour of it.

Humphries is on the crest of a wave at present and lifting another trophy at the weekend, winning the European Grand Prix in Stuttgart after beating Rob Cross 8-7 in the final.
