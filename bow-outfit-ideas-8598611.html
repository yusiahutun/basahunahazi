13 Bow Outfit Ideas That Nail the Dreamy Trend

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, March 24, 2024

While we’ve seen a slew of trends make their mark this year, one ultra-femme style has pulled ahead of the pack. If the red carpet looks and runway trends of 2024 are any indication, bow outfits featuring everything from dainty ribbon stacks to single statement bows are set to dominate this year’s fashion trend cycle. Those of us who’ve waited patiently for this aesthetically romantic style to take hold so we can bust out our favorite boots and clothes are rejoicing.

Even if your closet is currently bereft of ribbons, you can still get in on the trend in a way that stays true to your personal style. The best thing about bow outfits is that they can be over-the-top or subtly understated. Since bows are relatively simple to pull (and tie) off, designers like Sandy Liang, Nana Jacqueline, and Simone Rocha have room to get creative with this trend’s design and execution.

Ahead, we’ve rounded up 13 bow outfits that capture the dreamy romanticism of this adornment thanks to beribboned blouses, mini-dresses, and stunning separates. 

Embrace Asymmetry

If you find perfectly symmetrical bow outfits to be a bit too much, you'll likely love the edginess that asymmetry brings to this trend. Look for bow accents that sit at the hip or shoulder for a slightly off-kilter and elevated vibe.

Generally, the sleeker the bow, the more understated the effect will be, while soft colors and gauzy fabrics have a more over-the-top and romantic impact.

Slip Into Sheer

Bows' big femme energy lends itself to gauzy, romantic fabrics like chiffon and tulle, making bow outfits a natural fit for playing up sheer dressing. If you've shied away from sheers for want of coverage, a relaxed fit sheer dress or top with strategically placed bows might be right for you.

Keep It Effortless and Elevated

If you live and breathe elevated separates, we recommend leaning into the pieces you already love, a neutral color palette, and effortless textiles like cotton and linen. When worn with a refined pair of trousers, even a top that's 80% bow can feel expertly understated. When it comes to bow slacks or jeans, smaller bows placed along seams or hemlines create an air of sophistication while still staying on trend.

Bow Big or Bow Home

When it comes to statement-making bow outfits, it doesn't get easier than a blouse or top with a big bow front and center. If your wardrobe features a signature fabric like sheers, lace, or metallics, adding a bow top is a great way to test out the trend while staying true to your aesthetic.

Step Into Bow Shoes

Turn your favorite OOTD into a bow outfit by adding a pair of heels or boots decorated with bows. Metallic bow footwear will give any look a modern edge while a pair in cream or black delivers a vintage, throwback vibe.

Test Out the Hosiery Hype

Start your bow outfit from the ground up by incorporating a pair of bow socks or tights. Not only does the bow trend's innate daintiness lend itself to hosiery, but stockings and socks deliver maximum bow bang without upending your budget.

Try a Mini Dress with Mini Bows

Short hemlines and delicate bows go together like pastels and springtime. One of the most understated bow outfits in this roundup, a mini dress with more structure than flounce adorned with a few delicate bows feels romantic without being too sweet.

Throw It Back

Bows look great from every angle, but we love the dramatic reveal of a bow outfit that buries the lede by placing the signature design detail on the back of the look. A cape-style gown is a high-impact formalwear take on the trend, while a sleek pencil skirt with a bow-embellished peplum adds a touch of sly romance.

Consider a Skirt

For a more unique approach to bow outfits, target the trend from the waist down. A bow skirt, no matter its length, offers ample opportunity to seamlessly blend the bow trend into your favorite aesthetics. A floral blouse and colorful heels will amp up the ultra-femme energy while streetwear staples like a faux fur jacket or loafers add edge.

Add an Outer Layer

A great way to test-drive a bow outfit without fully committing to the trend is by adding a bow jacket, sweater, or blazer to your look. Bow or ribbon closures offer an elevated approach while playfully placed bows and mixed textiles veer toward the eclectic.

Try a Face Framing Necktie

Neckties have a rich sartorial history, particularly in menswear, and we love the vintage vibes that a face-framing bow top or dress can bring to a look. Evoke luxurious formalwear with a black and white palette or keep things retro and whimsical with an idyllic print.

Try a Matching Set

If you live, laugh, love bows, maximize the trend by donning a matching set that features bows on both the top and bottom pieces. You can go subtle with a handful of bows or push the trend to its limit with stacks of them.

Bust Out a Bow Belt

Bow belts are another great option for anyone on the fence about investing in bow-covered clothing. For more elevated looks, try a waist-cinching basque belt. You can also opt to keep things casual with a soft and silky belt.
