With Christmas just weeks away we've teamed up with some amazing brands to help you out with some gift ideas for your loved ones.

Entries open from today (Wednesday, 1 November) and will close on Thursday, November 30. It's completely free to enter but before you do be sure to read the competition Terms and Conditions at the bottom of this article.

Muscle Food

Muscle Food’s hampers allow customers to take away the hassle of last-minute food shopping by having their Christmas roast ingredients delivered straight to the front door.

  • 6 x 66g Meaty Pork Sausages
  • 1 x 300g Low Fat Bacon Medallions

Muscle Food is delivering Christmas hampers up until the 23rd of December - just in time for the big festive day!

Snugel

Snugel is giving away X2 cocooning long hot water bottles and X2 heated blankets. With the colder nights rolling in this is the perfect gift for your loved one this Christmas.

As well as keeping you warm over winter the Snugel hot water bottle can also be used to cool down your body down on hot days and nights or after an intense workout. Apply the Snugel bottle around your neck and under your arms to cool your internal body temperature. It can also be used to help ease any swelling or inflammation on your muscles or joints through targeted ice therapy.

The Snugel extra thick heated blanket has been designed as a heavier superior heated throw. Ultra-safe heating elements woven into the soft-touch sherpa fabric ensure you are kept warmer for longer.The blanket features 6 fully adjustable temperature settings giving you complete heating flexibility. All settings are controlled by the simple and easy-to-use remote control and a built-in 8-hour timer makes it perfect for use on the sofa or when falling asleep.

Trentham Monkey Forest

If you fancy a day out with the family making wonderful memories then Trentham Monkey Forest has you covered. They are giving away eight tickets for you and your loved ones to visit the forest where 140 monkeys roam freely.

The woodland is the monkey’s tranquil home in Trentham and the conservation site is dedicated to the protection of the highly endangered Barbary macaque monkeys. The primates can go wherever they like as they aren’t restricted by any bars or cages, allowing visitors to observe the species in a unique way that can’t be done anywhere else in the UK.

The forest is based in Stoke-On-Trent and has lots of play equipment outside of the Monkey Forest, as well as a brand-new monkey-shaped Willow Maze, encouraging visitors to spend their whole day at the attraction.
