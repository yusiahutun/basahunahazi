Clint Capela – Professional Life

Clint Capela made his debut for the Swiss national basketball team during the FIBA EuroBasket 2015 qualification rounds in August 2013. In 2014, he was traded to the Houston Rockets.

Clint joined the senior Elan Chalon team of LNB Pro A in 2012. In addition, he participated in the 2014 Nike Hoop Summit for the World Team. Meanwhile, he declared for the NBA draft in 2014. Clint was also named the Best Young Player and the Most Improved Player of the 2013-14 LNB Pro A Season in May 2014.

Clint signed a deal with the Rockets in July 2014. In the Rockets’ victory over the Charlotte Hornets, he also had his best game of the season with 10 points and five rebounds. In comparison, he played in more playoff games than regular-season games.

Likewise, in a 116-110 win over the Sacramento Kings on November 6, 2015, he posted his first career double with 13 points and 12 rebounds as a starter. Clint also played in the Rising Stars Challenge for the World Team at the 2016 NBA All-Star Weekend.

On November 19, 2016, he set a career-high with 20 points in a victory over the Utah Jazz. Meanwhile, he set a new career-high with 21 points against the Portland Trail Blazers. Later, he was forced to leave the game due to a fractured fibula, missing 15 games. Capela also set a career-high with 22 points and 17 rebounds in a 105-100 victory over the Sacramento Kings on October 18, 2017. In a 130-104 victory over the Denver Nuggets on February 9, 2018, he had 23 points and a career-high 25 rebounds. Clint later became the youngest Rocket to score at least 23 points and grab at least 25 rebounds.

In addition, after scoring career highs of 13.9 points in 2017-2918, he was runner-up for the NBA Most Improved Player Award. Clint became a restricted free agent after the 2017-18 season. He signed a five-year, $90 million contract extension with the Rockets in July of 2018.
