Royal Wharf, London E16 | South China Morning Post

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, July 20, 2024

[Sponsored Article] With approximately 500m of direct south facing river frontage, Royal Wharf offers high-quality waterside living with stunning panoramic views. The wealth of interconnecting green spaces, and the lively mosaic of shops, cafés, bars and restaurants, will form a backdrop to the true heart of Royal Wharf: over 3,300 contemporary homes spread across the entire 40 acre site.

The scale of Royal Wharf offers the potential to create a new great estate for London. Not only does Royal Wharf offer a magnificent south facing riverside promenade, but also has at its heart a new vibrant high street connecting parks and squares. There are spectecular apartment buildings offering unrivalled views of the Thames, terraced mansion blocks offering generous family sized apartments and a range of townhouses with tall ceilings and gorgeous private gardens.

Royal Wharf is already being serviced by some of the best public infrastructure in London including the Thames Barrier Park, Docklands Light Railway (DLR), Jubilee Line and London City Airport. Upon the arrival of the new Crossrail station (opening 2018), it will bring Royal Wharf's already excellent transportation connectivity to a whole new level.

With the £1 billion deal between ABP China and the London government in place, Royal Albert Docks will be transformed into the capital’s third business district – a commercial and trading centre that will deliver more than 20,000 employment opportunities in Newham.

Royal Wharf – the 40 acres of riverside land, at the epicenter of London’s blossoming east – creates an unrivalled opportunity that is not to be missed.
