Linfield FC is one of the oldest football clubs in Northern Ireland.

Established in 1886, Linfield FC has a rich history that spans over a century. The club has consistently been a dominant force in Northern Irish football, with a record number of league titles and domestic trophies.

The team’s home stadium is Windsor Park.

Windsor Park, located in Belfast, serves as the home ground for Linfield FC. With a seating capacity of over 18,000, it provides an electric atmosphere for fans and players alike.

Linfield FC has a fierce rivalry with Glentoran FC.

The heated rivalry between Linfield FC and Glentoran FC is one of the most intense in Northern Irish football. Matches between the two teams often result in sold-out crowds and passionate displays of support.

Linfield FC’s success in the Irish League is unparalleled. With a staggering 54 league titles to their name, they have established themselves as the most successful club in Northern Irish football history.

The team colors of Linfield FC are royal blue and white.

Royal blue and white have been the traditional colors of Linfield FC since its inception. The team’s iconic blue jerseys, paired with white shorts and socks, create a visually striking look on the field.

Linfield FC has a long-standing tradition of producing talented youth players.

The club has a renowned youth academy that has produced several notable players who have gone on to have successful careers both domestically and internationally.

Linfield FC has represented Northern Ireland in European competitions.

As one of the top teams in Northern Ireland, Linfield FC has had the opportunity to compete in European competitions such as the UEFA Champions League and the UEFA Europa League.

Linfield FC holds the record for the longest unbeaten run in Irish League history.

In the 2011-2012 season, Linfield FC went an impressive 31 league games without suffering a defeat, setting a new record in Irish League football.

The loyal supporters of Linfield FC are known for their unwavering devotion to the club. Matchdays at Windsor Park are always filled with vibrant chants and a sea of blue and white scarves.
