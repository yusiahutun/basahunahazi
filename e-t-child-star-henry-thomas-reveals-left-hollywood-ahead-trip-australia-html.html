E.T. child star Henry Thomas reveals why he left Hollywood ahead of his trip to Australia

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, September 26, 2024

He shot to stardom with his leading role in E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial 41 years ago.

And now, Henry Thomas has spoken to Yahoo Entertainment about growing up in the spotlight and why he had to take a break from Hollywood.

The actor, 51, explained that his decided to leave Los Angeles and relocate to Oregon to enjoy a quieter life.

'I loved L.A., but wasn't really working there,' he shared, adding he would often drive from Hollywood to a picturesque farm in Oregon.

This continuous cycle finally drove him to say, 'I'm not going to do this anymore.'

E.T. star Henry Thomas, 51, (right) spoke about growing up in the spotlight and why he had to take a break from Hollywood. Pictured with co-star Dee Wallace

Speaking to Yahoo Entertainment, the now 51-year-old explained that after growing up on a farm, he wanted to give his kids the same experience

'I grew up on a farm and I wanted to give my kids that experience,' the actor explained, hinting at his desire for a quieter, more grounded life.

Thomas shares his new homestead with his wife Annalee Fery and their two children, along with a daughter from a previous relationship.

Thomas played the role of Elliott in E.T. and his character has long been a part of Hollywood lore.

Iconic: The 1982 science fiction film surpassed Star Wars to become the highest-grossing film of all time - a record it held for eleven years until Jurassic Park was released in 1993

The audition that moved Spielberg to tears and won him the part is now a cherished piece of film history.

Recollecting that life-changing moment, Thomas shares, 'Honestly, I think I had the part before I went into the audition. I got very emotional during the improv, and at the end, Spielberg simply said: 'OK kid, you got the job'.' 

Australia will get a dose of nostalgia as Thomas flies down for his first pop culture expo at Melbourne's Metro Comic Con on July 8-9.
