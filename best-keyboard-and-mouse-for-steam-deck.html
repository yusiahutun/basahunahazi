Valve really knocked it out of the park with the Steam Deck, given its performance, handheld form factor, and aggressive pricing. The triggers feel nice, as do the analog sticks, and there are some handy buttons at the back you can map desired keys to. But if you want to unlock the Steam Deck's full power, you need to grab a keyboard and mouse.

It is a handheld PC, after all. Thanks to its built-in Bluetooth connectivity, you can quickly switch to a keyboard and mouse when the need arises, turning your Steam Deck into a makeshift gaming desktop.

If you want to enhance the impressive hardware the right way, you need to pick up the best keyboard and mice for Steam Deck.

1. Corsair Harpoon RGB Wireless

When it’s time to forego the Steam Deck’s joysticks for a mouse, you in no way want to put yourself at a disadvantage, especially if you’re jumping into a multiplayer title. To keep the good times going, and your performance at its peak, the Corsair Harpoon RGB Wireless Gaming Mouse has the design and durability to back you up.

The last thing you want is a mouse that doesn’t register your clicks and movements, right? Thanks to Corsair’s Slipstream wireless technology, the Corsair Harpoon RGB Wireless Gaming Mouse reaches a latency as low as 1ms. To put that in perspective, that’s the kind of low-latency you’d expect from a wired mouse.

Performance aside, you’re going to love how the Corsair Harpoon RGB Wireless Gaming Mouse feels to use. The feet, for example, glide wonderfully across mousepads, on top of being quite lightweight at just 99 grams. Best of all, the shape has a nice curve to it that cradles your hands, along with rubberized siding to give your fingers something to grip to.

FAQ

Q: Should I Purchase a Keyboard and Mouse for My Steam Deck?

Given how the Steam Deck is designed, you don't have to stress over the need for a keyboard and mouse.

With that said, by pairing the Steam Deck with the Steam Deck Dock, it turns into a capable gaming PC. There are plenty of titles that would benefit greatly with a much larger screen, in addition to a keyboard and mouse, like an RTS or MMO.

Q: Is the Steam Deck Compatible With Any Keyboard and Mouse?

Yes and no.

The Steam Deck alone doesn't feature any USB ports for plugging a keyboard and mouse directly, but it does come with Bluetooth 5.0. As long as you have a Bluetooth keyboard and mouse, you're good to go. If you want to go the wired route, you'll have to get a Steam Deck Dock or a USB-C hub.

Q: What Other Accessories Should I Purchase for My Steam Deck?

If you haven't already picked up a Steam Deck carrying case, it's one of the first accessories you should invest in.

Outside of that, you can't go wrong with a portable power bank. The Steam Deck offers anywhere between 2 to 8 hours of battery life, which is fine for the commute home, but some extra juice never hurt.
