36 Elizabeth Taylor Style Moments 2023: See Photos Here

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, June 16, 2024
1

1946: Signing Autographs

Hulton Archive//Getty Images

Taylor wore a ruffled white dress with matching gloves to sign autographs for fans waiting outside a nightclub.

2

1948: Flashing a Smile

Silver Screen Collection//Getty Images

The actress pulled out all the stops for the camera in a dark lip, an ivory dress with pleated detailing, and drop pearl earrings.

3

1948: A Date with Judy

Donaldson Collection//Getty Images

Taylor posed for a portrait on the set of A Date with Judy while dressed in an off-the-shoulder black blouse with a floral printed skirt. The outfits for the movie were designed by Helen Rose, an award-winning costume designer who also created the looks for The Bad and the Beautiful and Cat on a Hot Tin Roof.

4

1948: Pretty in Pink

Screen Archives//Getty Images

The Hollywood star is pictured here sitting in bed while dressed in a pastel pink pajama set. The headboard and comforter complement her outfit perfectly.

5

1949: With Her Parents in New York City

Bettmann//Getty Images

Taylor wore a lacy white top with black ribbon detailing while enjoying lunch with her parents at New York City's Stork Club in 1949.

6

1950: Mesh Details

Hulton Archive//Getty Images

She wore a bold mesh look for a photoshoot, complete with a white hat and a matching suit, in 1950.

7

1951: A Place in the Sun

Hulton Archive//Getty Images

Taylor flashed a big smile on the set of A Place In the Sun dressed in an elegant strapless silk gown. The costumes for the film were designed by Edith Head, a famed costume designer who also did the looks for Sabrina and Roman Holiday.

8

1951: See-Through Handbag

Keystone-France//Getty Images

The actress showed off her modern, see-through handbag at London's Savoy Hotel.

9

1952: Love is Better Than Ever

George Rinhart//Getty Images

She sported a dark leotard and fishnet tights during a promotional photoshoot for Love Is Better Than Ever.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

1953: Beauty Queen

Sunset Boulevard//Getty Images

The movie star stepped up her beauty game with winged eyeliner, coiffed hair, and statement earrings. Just when we thought she couldn’t be more fabulous!

11

1953: Crystal Details

Keystone//Getty Images

Taylor is seen here with her young son Michael. She decided to wear a dashing pillbox hat with a head-turning crystal broach pinned to it.

12

1954: An Embroidered Dress

Silver Screen Collection//Getty Images

While lounging against a couch, Taylor went bridal with an intricately embroidered white dress styled with a pair of diamond earrings.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

13

1954: A Star is Born

Hulton Archive//Getty Images

Taylor walked the red carpet for the A Star Was Born premiere in an extravagant lace and silk gown with an oversized white stole. She attended the event with her second husband, Michael Wilding. The two were married from 1952 to 1957.

14

1955: Giant

Archive Photos//Getty Images

Taylor took a break between filming scenes on the set of Giant dressed in a pale pink scarf, a loose-fitting blouse, and a pleated gray skirt.

15

1955: Signature Style

Silver Screen Collection//Getty Images

Sometimes, less is more, and that’s exactly what Taylor did here. In the mid-'50s, the actress donned a simple black halter-neck dress and statement-making diamond earrings.

16

1955: Mellow Yellow

Silver Screen Collection//Getty Images

Then again, there’s nothing wrong with a pop of color like Taylor’s furry stole against a buttery yellow backdrop here.

17

1955: With her Pet Poodle

Silver Screen Collection//Getty Images

That same year, she wowed fans in this hot pink strapless number styled with a shiny diamond choker. Funny enough, she had all this on to just feed her adorable poodle.

18

1956: Taffeta & Tiaras

George Rinhart//Getty Images

She kept the strapless silhouette going but elevated it this time with a floral tiara.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

19

1957: Raintree Country

Herbert Dorfman//Getty Images

The star looked lovely in a white eyelet dress featuring a subtle baby blue ribbon while on set for the 1957 romance drama Raintree Country. Oscar-winning costume designer Walter Plunkett—also known for his looks in An American in Paris, Gone With the Wind, and Singin’ in the Rain—worked on the film.

20

1957: Academy Awards

Screen Archives//Getty Images

Taylor attended the 1957 Academy Awards with her third husband Mike Todd. She made a grand entrance in a flowy empire waist chiffon gown and, of course, a tiara.
