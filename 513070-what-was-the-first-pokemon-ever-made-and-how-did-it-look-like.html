The history of Pokémon

Pokémon was released in its home country, Japan, in 1996, and it debuted in the United States of America in August 1998. The game gained global attention fast.

Players assume the role of Pokémon trainers, obtaining cartoon monsters and developing them to battle Pokémon. People liked the narrative, so the game is one of the world's most successful franchises, second only to Super Mario Bros.

At some point, the Pokémon craze was so rampant in the United Kingdom that students were banned from trading Pokémon cards. After the first movie, Pokémon: The First Movie, debuted in the United States of America, it gained massive attention from children, and the New York Times dubbed it the “Pokeflu".

The world of Pokémon has three primary goals. The first goal is to travel and explore new areas, while the second is to train a small team of Pokémon. The third is to discover and catch a specimen of each Pokémon species. And that is what makes the game fun and engaging, bringing about a worldwide craze.

What was the first Pokemon ever made?

There are thousands of Pokémon today. And with numerous classic Pokémon in the first generation of games, you might be trying to figure out which one was created first. Probably Pikachu, one of the most popular, comes to mind. Pikachu became iconic because of the anime. But was it the first?

Interestingly, the first Pokémon ever created was not Pikachu. This revelation is according to designer Ken Sugimori. And to make it even worse, Pikachu wasn’t even among the starter Pokémon or their evolutions, and it was not even a Legendary.

You might be surprised to know that the first ever Pokémon was Rhydon, number 112. Initially, the idea behind Pokémon designs was to create something mimicking dinosaurs, and Rhydon’s design perfectly depicts this ideology. The Pokémon has a long tail, horns, and a dinosaur-like body.

The first Pokémon were Rhydon, Clefairy, and Lapras. At first, we'd planned to have Pokémon living alongside humans, making their lives easier. So, during the early stages of development, many of the characters we came up with had clear roles, like carrying things around or sailing across the sea with people on their backs. We also thought up cute pet-like characters like Clefairy.

The game's creators later created Pokémon designs to look like animals and some inanimate objects.

Another way to learn more about the first Pokémon is to look back at the gym in one of the early Pokémon games or the statues littered throughout other locations. You will notice that all the statues are of Rhydon.

What were the original Pokémon?

The first generation of Pokémon of the Pokémon franchise consists of the original 151 fictional species introduced in the 1996 Game Boy games Pokémon Red and Blue. The first one is Bulbasaur, and the second one is Ivysaur. Pikachu is also part of the original list and is number 25.

What is the first Pokémon in the Pokédex?

The first Pokémon in the Pokédex is Bulbasaur. The Pokédex is a comprehensive, electronic reference encyclopaedia often referred to to deliver information necessary to the plot.

What was the first Pokémon game?

The first-ever Pokémon game in the franchise is Pokémon Green and Pokémon Red. These games were released in Japan in February 1996.

What was the first Pokemon card ever made?

The 1998 Pokémon Demo Game Plastic Pack were the earliest card released in the English Pokémon TCG. These cards served as the introduction of Pokémon cards in the United States of America.

Is Mew the first Pokemon?

Mew is number 151 in the National Pokédex, the last of the first-generation Pokémon. According to Ken Sugimori, the Pokémon was not the first, even though a fan theory suggests that the Pokémon was the first.

Is Arceus the first Pokémon?

Arceus is one of the original Pokémon but not the first. The first was Rhydon, created when the designer wanted all Pokémon designs to borrow from dinosaurs. The design ideology later changed, and they adopted other animals and objects.
