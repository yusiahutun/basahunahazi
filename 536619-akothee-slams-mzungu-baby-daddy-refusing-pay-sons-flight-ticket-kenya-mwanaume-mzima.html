Akothee Slams Mzungu Baby Daddy for Refusing to Pay Son's Flight Ticket to Kenya: &quot;Mwanaume Mzima&quot;

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, August 28, 2024
  • Singer Akothee narrated the challenges she has gone through with her baby daddies while noting the struggle of co-parenting to
  • The mother of five recalled her Swiss baby daddy refusing to pay tickets for their son to Nairobi despite a court order and said she asked her other mzungu baby daddy to pay for them
  • She also added that she is so busy with Nelly Oaks that she has no time to fight her baby daddies as she does not let them hurt her

Veteran singer Akothee has given women co-parenting with their baby daddies tips to help them keep while narrating her personal experiences.

Akothee, who has three baby daddies, admitted that no woman does not cry monthly because of what the baby daddy is doing or has done to them.

Akothee on taking first baby daddy to court

In a Live video that was reshared on TikTok by @Annkusj411, the singer recalled her disagreements with her baby daddy.

"Co-parenting is not easy. There is no day a month will pass without you crying. I'm telling you the truth. If you meet a baby mama who is not being made to cry by the baby daddy or a baby daddy who is not being made to cry by the baby mama, it's a lie. As a baby mama, you will cry until the day the kids grow up. And you will still cry with them grown up. You will call that man to your kid's wedding, and he will refuse to come. Will that not hurt you?"

Akothee also recalled a time she took her daughters' father to court only for him to show up with evidence that left her feeling defeated.

"Some of us had to swallow our pride. We found out that pride does not work. I walked to the children's court thinking I am a mother. I have it all. The man came with an encyclopedia, showing he only earns KSh 9000 a month after all his deductions and he has another family. He defended himself. I was left in the light with three of my kids," she said.

How Akothee co-parents with Mzungu's baby daddy

Akothee recalled having a child with a mzungu from Swiss and how she cried until her tears dried.

"These days, I only watch with my eyes. I don't allow him to hurt him. Do not think he has stopped hurting me. He has not. It's because I have my own coins so I can cover a lot of things. When he refuses to pay flight tickets for the kid, it's him trying to upset me," she added.

She added that the 63-year-old only tells the kid that he cannot pay his flight ticket to see his mum rather than tell her.

"He expects me to call him so that we start insulting each other. I am so busy with Nelly Oaks I do not have time to insult him. I gave him a cold silence," she added.
