John Stamos wishes comedy legend Dick Van Dyke a very happy 95th birthday on social media

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, May 14, 2024

John Stamos took to Twitter on Sunday afternoon to wish a very happy birthday to comedy legend Dick Van Dyke.

Van Dyke celebrated his 95th birthday on Sunday, with Stamos sharing a photo of them both on Twitter.

The Full House star also gave the Hollywood legend a heartfelt birthday message along with the new snap.

Birthday wishes: John Stamos took to Twitter on Sunday afternoon to wish a very happy birthday to comedy legend Dick Van Dyke

'Happy 95th Birthday to this magnificent man,' Stamos began in his birthday greeting to Van Dyke.

'I've been fortunate enough to know him a little over the years and he's everything you'd want Dick Van Dyke to be!' Stamos added.

'Happy Happy @iammrvandy XO,' Stamos concluded the birthday message.

While Stamos and Van Dyke have never worked together on the big or small screen, they do share a role on the Broadway stage.

Van Dyke originated the role of Albert Pearson, the central character in the original Broadway production of Bye Bye Birdie in 1960. 

Nearly 50 years later, the play was given a revival, with Stamos portraying the iconic role of Albert Pearson.

Happy Vandy: 'Happy Happy @iammrvandy XO,' Stamos concluded the birthday message

The duo are also 'neighbors' of sorts, with their stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame literally right next to each other.

Van Dyke was awarded his star on the Walk of Fame back in February 1993, while Stamos' star was awarded in November 2009.

Both of the actors' stars reside outside 7201 Hollywood Blvd. in the heart of Hollywood, California.

Van Dyke still continues to work fairly regularly, coming off playing Mr. Dawes Jr. in Disney's Mary Poppins Returns and guest starring on Hopscotch.

Stamos returned as Uncle Jesse in the Netflix series Fuller House, bringing back the cast of the original 1980s hit series Full House.

His next role was announced last week at Disney Investor Day, playing basketball coach Marvyn Korn in the new series Big Shot, debuting next year on Disney Plus.
